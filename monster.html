Deathclaw

Originally engineered by the US government
before the Great War as a cheap
replacement for human troops during
combat operations, deathclaws were
bred from a mix of various animal
species, primarily the popular
Jackson's chameleon. The project
succeeded in creating a wild
predator capable of surviving in
the wilderness, but there are no
records of them ever being
deployed in combat. After the
Great War, deathclaws
escaped into the wild and
quickly spread across the
continent.

Feral ghoul

Ghouls in the Commonwealth are
humans transformed by radioactive
fallout from the Great War. Most
were pre-War survivors initially
exposed to nuclear bombardment,
leading to permanent irradiation,
decay, and disfigurement. Despite
this, their lifespans are extended
indefinitely. However, continued
radiation exposure in the
wasteland often deteriorates their
mental faculties, reducing them to
mindless, brcannibalistic beings with
enhanced radiation resistance.

Mirelurk

Mirelurks are among the
earliest radioactive mutants
to appear due to widespread
environmental contamination
by major US corporations like
Poseidon Energy and General
Atomics.

Super mutant

The super mutants of the
Commonwealth are distinct from
Vault 87 super mutants or those
created at Mariposa. They stand
more upright, and have intact
lips. While their speech is very
simple, they may speak casually,
unlike the strained speech of
mutants from Vault 87. They
consider themselves the superior
race of the Commonwealth.

Assaultron

The Assaultron, created by RobCo
Industries and supplied to the US
military from 2072 onwards, serves
as a frontline combatant. Known for its
speed and lethality in close quarters,
it wields a powerful laser for
long-range engagements. They
relentlessly pursue their targets,
fighting until their core is
compromised, even when
sustaining significant damage.
Some Assaultrons are outfitted
with self-destruct mechanisms
designed to activate upon destruction.

Protectron

A widespread and popular model
in RobCo Industries line of products,
the Protectron was designed as an
inexpensive work drone, capable of
performing a wide variety of tasks such
as construction, firefighting, and office
duties. Some models were designed
for security and have defensive
capabilities.

Synth

Synths are advanced humanoid robots
created by the Institute, a secretive
organization based in the ruins of
Boston. They are designed to mimic
human appearance and behavior,
making them indistinguishable from
real humans to the casual observer.
Synths can be employed for various
tasks, including infiltration, combat,
and scientific research, often causing
fear and suspicion among wasteland
inhabitants due to their potential to
infiltrate settlements and mimic loved
ones. Their creation sparks ethical debates
and conflict throughout the game's storyline.

Raider

Raiders are hostile human scavengers
and marauders who roam the wasteland,
preying on survivors and settlements.
They are known for their crude weapons,
makeshift armor, and ruthless tactics.
Raiders can be found in makeshift camps,
ruins, and other dilapidated structures
throughout the Commonwealth, posing a
constant threat to anyone they encounter.
They typically exhibit aggressive behavior
and will attack on sight, driven by a desire
for resources and power in the
post-apocalyptic brworld of Fallout 4.

Nuka-Cola

Nuka-Cola is the iconic soft drink in Fallout 4, highly sought after in the post-apocalyptic world. It comes in various variants such as Nuka-Cola Quantum or Nuka-Cherry, each with special effects. The advertising and design of Nuka-Cola evoke nostalgia from the 1950s.
